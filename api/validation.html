Dynamic-Treatment DGP (dml_ts.validation.dynamic_dgp)#

Panel and single-series data with known per-period treatment blips, for validating recursive dynamic g-estimation against ground truth.

Dynamic-treatment DGP with known period-specific blips for g-estimation.

Generates data with a known sequence of per-period treatment effects theta_t = (theta_1, ..., theta_m) so that recursive dynamic g-estimation (Lewis & Syrgkanis, 2021, arXiv:2002.07285) can be validated against ground truth. This is the missing piece: the repo’s existing DGPs expose only a scalar effect, fixed lag coefficients, or piecewise regimes – none a true sequence of period-specific blips.

Two settings (both supported):

  • mode="panel" – n i.i.d. trajectories, each of length m periods. This is the canonical Lewis-Syrgkanis setting (the structure needed to identify m separate blips) and is directly comparable to EconML’s DynamicDML (stacked rows + groups).

  • mode="series" – a single long, stationary series with distributed-lag treatment effects Y_t = sum_k theta_{k+1} T_{t-k} + .... This is the single-unit, stationarity-based adaptation that Lewis & Syrgkanis also provide (their “progressive nuisance estimation analogue”).

Structural model (panel), with optional treatment-dependent state:

X_{i,tau} = A X_{i,tau-1} + b * T_{i,tau-1} + eps (state, tau > 1) T_{i,tau} = pi . X_{i,tau} + eta (selection on state) Y_{i,tau} = sum_{s<=tau} theta_s * T_{i,s} + gamma . X_{i,tau} + u

When state_feedback=True the state depends on past treatment, so the total effect of T_tau on the final outcome includes the indirect path through future states. For the linear system above that total per-period blip is available in closed form,

theta*_tau = theta_tau + gamma^T A^{m-2-tau} b (tau <= m-2), theta*_{m-1} = theta_{m-1},

which is exactly the structural-nested-mean-model estimand that g-estimation (and EconML’s DynamicDML) recovers. DynamicTreatmentDGPResult.theta_t always holds this total blip, so tests compare estimates against the correct target regardless of the state_feedback setting.

Generate dynamic-treatment data with known per-period blips theta_t.

See the module docstring for the structural model. The generator is fully deterministic given random_state and, with noise_level=0 and linear nuisances, returns data whose blips are recovered exactly – the basis for the estimator’s deterministic-recovery test.

Parameters:

  • n_periods (int) – Horizon m (number of per-period blips). Must be >= 2.

  • theta_t (float | ndarray[tuple[Any, ...], dtype[double]]) – True per-period blips. Scalar (broadcast to all m periods) or a length-m array. Interpreted as the direct structural coefficients; the result exposes the total blip (equal to these when state_feedback=False).

  • n_units (int) – Number of i.i.d. trajectories (panel). Ignored for the series.

  • p (int) – Number of confounders.

  • state_feedback (bool) – If True, the state depends on past treatment (X_tau += b * T_{tau-1}), creating genuine time-varying confounding; the result’s theta_t is adjusted to the closed-form total effect. If False, the state is exogenous (matches EconML’s example convention and yields an unambiguous estimand).

  • state_transition (float | ndarray[tuple[Any, ...], dtype[double]]) – AR coefficient for the state – scalar (c * I) or a (p, p) matrix A.

  • treatment_state_coef (float | ndarray[tuple[Any, ...], dtype[double]] | None) – b – how past treatment shifts the state (scalar -> first coordinate, or length-p vector). Only used when state_feedback=True.

  • treatment_policy_coef (float | ndarray[tuple[Any, ...], dtype[double]] | None) – pi – selection of treatment on the current state (scalar -> first coordinate, or length-p vector).

  • confounding_coef (float | ndarray[tuple[Any, ...], dtype[double]] | None) – gamma – effect of the state on the outcome (scalar -> all coordinates, or length-p vector).

  • treatment_type (Literal['continuous', 'binary']) – "continuous" or "binary".

  • noise_level (float) – Default std for the state, treatment, and outcome noise terms (used wherever a specific channel below is left None).

  • state_noise (float | None) – Std of the state shock eps (defaults to noise_level).

  • treatment_noise (float | None) – Std of the treatment shock eta (defaults to noise_level). This is the independent treatment variation that identifies the blips; it must be > 0 for recovery.

  • outcome_noise (float | None) – Std of the outcome shock u (defaults to noise_level). Set to 0 for an exact (deterministic) recovery test.

  • series_length (int) – Length of the single series for mode="series".

  • mode (Literal['panel', 'series']) – "panel" (default) or "series".

  • random_state (int | None) – Seed for reproducibility.

Raises:
